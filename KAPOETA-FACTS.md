## 1. Founding & Mission

- **Founder name and title:** Brother Hakim Peter (referred to as "Brother" — not "Father", not "Pastor") (Vision doc)
- **Founder's background:** A native of Kapoeta who had migrated to the United States (Vision doc). NOTE: the Vision doc says "United States" — see Section 10 for website discrepancy.
- **Founding trigger:** Received a "divine calling" to support his community; responded "with obedience and compassion" (Vision doc)
- **Land:** Granted a parcel of land from local authorities at no cost (Vision doc)
- **Location:** Kapoeta, South Sudan (all docs)
- **Land area:** 10,000 square metres (100 m x 100 m) owned by Triple L Orphanage and Vulnerable Children Organization (Final Report, Letter to Aman sponsors, Vision doc)
- **Original activity:** Gathered street children during the day to teach biblical stories and hymns, provide one meal, offer a safe space; children slept on streets near the local market at night (Vision doc)
- **Children described before shelter:** Wearing tattered clothes, eating from trash bins, no access to education (Vision doc)
- **Initial number of children encountered:** 150–200 kids aged 2–18 years old (MEPC letter)
- **Capacity planned:** 150 children (UK fundraising letter, Bahrain letter)
- **Children needing sponsorship at time of writing:** 45 total; 10 already sponsored (Vision doc, Orphanage Update summary)
- **Mission operating under:** St. Mark Nubian Foundation — a registered charity in Australia (multiple docs)

## 11. Discrepancies / Open Questions

1. **Container arrival date:** Final Report and St. Mark Final Report say container "arrived on 11-1-2024" (which could mean 11 January 2024 or 11 November 2024). The Letter to Aman sponsors (Oct 28, 2024) says "expected to arrive on 1-11-2024" (1 November 2024). The fundraising only started May 2024 and the container was packed mid-2024, so 11 January 2024 is almost certainly a typo — most likely 11 January 2025 or 11 November 2024. **Needs clarification.**

2. **Number of cows:** Vision doc says "5 cows and a young bull." Final Report and letters to sponsors consistently say "7 cows." These may refer to different purchase moments, or one figure includes the bull. **Needs clarification.**

3. **Wheelchairs:** Final Report and most letters say "6 wheelchairs." Cost and Weights PDF says "7 wheel chairs received." **Minor discrepancy — needs clarification.**

4. **Bunk beds:** Letters and Final Report say "33 bunk beds" in the container. Cost and Weights PDF shows 20 + 12 = 32 bunk beds received. **Minor discrepancy.**

5. **Hakim Peter's location — US vs Australia:** Vision doc clearly states "United States." No document says Australia. Website may have an error if it says Australia anywhere. The "Vision doc" is the primary source and says US.

6. **"Presbyterian churches"** vs general "churches": Final Report and Letter to Aman sponsors (English) say "Presbyterian churches." St. Mark Final Report (separate document) says just "some churches." The Arabic Letter to Aman says "evangelical churches" (كنائس إنجيلية). These may refer to the same group described differently.

7. **Founding year:** No document gives a year for when Hakim Peter began his street-children activities. The fence, well and small building pre-date the 2024 container — described as done "last year" — suggesting approximately 2023 for that infrastructure. When Hakim Peter originally began is not stated. The claim "2020" on the website has no documentary support.

8. **"Toongabbie Church" name:** Documents call it "Toongabbie Church" or "Toongabi Church." The full denomination name is not given in any document. Vision doc credits it for funding the water well. Whether it is "Evangelical" or another denomination is not stated.

9. **Container delivery cost discrepancy:** Final Report shows AU$10,125.00 for delivery and AU$7,850.00 for clearance/transfer. St. Mark Final Report shows AU$10,125.00 delivery and AU$7,799.70 clearance. Minor $50.30 difference.

10. **"The Vision Expands" — Pastor Aman's visit:** Vision doc says "Pastor Aman—a Sudanese national—visited the area and was deeply moved." This suggests Pastor Aman visited Kapoeta. But Vision doc also says he is based in the US ("supported by Pastor Aman in the US"). Whether this was a visit from the US or he was in the region at the time is ambiguous.

11. **"A dedicated and loving servant currently leads these spiritual activities"** — this person is unnamed in the Vision doc, though Evangelist Simon is named separately as spiritual ministry leader. May be the same person or a different person for the Sunday services specifically.

12. **No dollar figure given for water tower or chicken coop** — website figures of $12,500 each are not in any source document and cannot be verified.

13. **Annual operating cost of $45,000** — not stated in any document. Cannot be verified.

14. **"$36,000 sponsor goal"** — 45 × AU$600 = AU$27,000. The $36,000 figure does not appear in any document.
